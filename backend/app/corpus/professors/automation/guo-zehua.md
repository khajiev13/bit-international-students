## 研究方向(Research Interests)
1. Software-Defined Networking and Network Function Virtualization
2. Cloud Computing and Data Center Networks
3. Machine Learning

## 教育背景(Educational Background)
- Prof. Guo received the B.S. degree from Northwestern Polytechnical University, China, the M.S. degree from Xidian University, China, and the Ph.D. degree from Northwestern Polytechnical University, China.

## 工作经历(Working Experience)
- Prof. Guo is an Associate Professor at School of Automation, Beijing Institute of Technology.
- He was a Visiting Research Scholar and a Research Fellow at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, New York University Tandon School of Engineering, USA, and a Postdoctoral Research Associate at University of Minnesota Twin Cities.

## 学术兼职(Part-time Academic Jobs)
- Prof. Guo is serving in the editorial board of several journals (e.g., IEEE Access, Springer EURASIP Journal on Wireless Communications and Networking, KSII Transactions on Internet and Information Systems, and Elsevier Journal of Parallel and Distributed Computing with Special issue on Enabling Technologies for Energy Cloud) and in the Technical Program Committee of several conferences (e.g., Elsevier Computer Communications, IEEE GLOBECOM 2019 Workshop).
- He is also a reviewer for 34 journals and 17 conferences.

## 发表文章(Publications)
- Prof. Guo has published 40 papers in prestigious journals (e.g., IEEE Journal on Selected Areas in Communications, IEEE Transactions on Network and Service Management, IEEE Systems Journal, IEEE Access, IEEE Communications Letters, Elsevier Computer Networks, Elsevier Journal of Parallel and Distributed Computing, Elsevier Future Generation Computer Systems, Elsevier Computer Communications) and prestigious conferences (e.g., IEEE/ACM International Symposium on Quality of Service, IEEE International Conference on Communications, IEEE Conference on Local Computer Networks, IEEE Symposium on Computers and Communications, ACM Special Interest Group on Data Communication, IEEE International Conference on Computer Communications).
